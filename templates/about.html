The Ancient Science

About Vedic Astrology

Jyotish — the eye of the Vedas — is one of humanity's oldest sciences of consciousness. Developed over 5,000 years in ancient India, it maps the relationship between celestial rhythms and human experience with extraordinary precision.

Foundation

What is Jyotish?

Jyotish (Sanskrit: ज्योतिष, "science of light") is the Vedic system of astrology. Unlike Western astrology which uses the tropical zodiac aligned with the seasons, Jyotish uses the sidereal zodiac — actual star positions — corrected by the Ayanamsa (currently ~24°) to account for the precession of equinoxes.

The result is a system that is astronomically grounded, highly personalised through the Ascendant (Lagna), and capable of specific timing through the Dasha planetary period system. Major life events — marriage, career shifts, illness, spiritual awakening — reliably correlate with Dasha transitions.

🪐
Graha (Planets)

Nine celestial bodies — Sun, Moon, Mars, Mercury, Jupiter, Venus, Saturn, Rahu and Ketu — each govern specific life domains and psychological functions.

🏠
Bhava (Houses)

Twelve houses represent every area of human life from personality (1st) and wealth (2nd) through to marriage (7th), career (10th) and liberation (12th).

♈
Rashi (Signs)

Twelve sidereal zodiac signs filter planetary energies. A planet's sign placement determines its mode of expression and how it interacts with the chart.

⭐
Nakshatra (Stars)

27 lunar mansions of 13°20' each provide the most granular layer of chart analysis — governing emotional nature, instinctive responses and karmic patterns.

⏱
Dasha (Timing)

The 120-year Vimshottari Dasha system maps life chapters to specific planetary periods. This is the primary timing mechanism — uniquely powerful in Jyotish.

✦
Yoga (Combinations)

Over 300 named planetary combinations (Yogas) indicate specific talents, destinies and life patterns — from Raj Yoga (power) to Dharma-Karma Adhipati Yoga.
